Treatments

Delivered through syringes, pens, pumps or new artificial pancreas systems. Frequency and type of insulin dosage vary on each individual.

Being aware of your blood-sugar levels and acting accordingly is important since monitoring helps individuals know when insulin is needed to correct high blood sugar and when carbohydrates are needed to correct low blood sugar.

High blood pressure and high cholesterol medications can be prescribed with insulin to help the overall health and treatment of diabetes.

Factoring the insulin to carb ratio helps maintain stable blood-sugar levels.

Eat a healthy mix of all food groups with a focus on lower consumption of empty carbs.
Engage in regular exercise as it helps control blood glucose because it increases your insulin sensitivity

Meet regularly with a team of medical professionals that include endocrinologists, ophthalmologists, and dietitians
